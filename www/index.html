How to chant mala

  1. Say the three pranam mantras.
  2. Press Pranams Done beside the beads.
  3. Chant one maha-mantra per bead.
  4. Complete 108 beads for one mala.

Help

Help

Dashboard

Set your chanting pledge, sloka pledge, and sloka pledge duration. Sloka pledges are counted only for today, so you cannot finish future days in advance.

Mala Counter

Press Pranams Done before each mala, then tap the japa beads. The bead count goes from 108 to 0 and resets each day.

Books

Open Books, choose a book, chapter, and sloka. Use search to search the current page or current section, then press Done at the bottom of a sloka for today's sloka pledge.

About

About

Journey to Vaikunta

A simple devotional companion for daily japa, chanting pledges, scripture study, sloka pledges, and monthly progress.

Privacy

Your counter, pledges, and reading progress are saved on this device in local app storage.

Book Text

The Books section includes Bhagavad-gita slokas and translations, Srimad-Bhagavatam slokas/translations, and Sri Caitanya-caritamrta original text. BBT English translations, synonyms, and purports are not bundled unless separately licensed.

Book Sources

Book navigation and source links point to Bhaktivedanta VedaBase pages for Bhagavad-gita As It Is, Sri Caitanya-caritamrta, and Srimad-Bhagavatam. Bhagavad-gita sloka text, transliteration, and English translation are from the gita/gita JSON dataset released under the Unlicense.

Srimad-Bhagavatam

English verse translations are bundled from Anand Aadhar's Srimad Bhagavatam: The Story of the Fortunate One, available at bhagavata.org / srimadbhagavatam.org under Creative Commons Attribution NonCommercial ShareAlike 3.0. Sanskrit sloka text/transliteration uses JDhruv14/Srimad_Bhagavatam and snskrt/Shrimad_Bhagvat_Puran Hugging Face datasets under Apache-2.0, plus bhagavata.org Sanskrit line text as fallback. This means attribution is required, commercial use is not allowed for the CC BY-NC-SA translation, and adaptations must use the same or a similar license.

Sri Caitanya-caritamrta

The app includes Sri Caitanya-caritamrta original Bengali/Sanskrit text for the verse pages. The original work by Krsnadasa Kaviraja Gosvami is public domain; VedaBase pages are used for navigation/reference. English BBT translations, synonyms, and purports are not bundled. The public-domain-in-the-USA selections from Chaitanya's Life And Teachings by Sir Jadunath Sarkar via Project Gutenberg ebook 38016 are included separately.
